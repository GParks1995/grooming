Front Desk Grooming Guide

This page is for our front desk team. Use it to learn the basics of our grooming offerings, booking process and how to speak confidently with clients about Leila’s services.

Booking Workflow

Follow these steps to create a seamless experience for our guests:

  • Ask the client for the dog’s breed, weight and coat length. Use the estimator tool to get an initial quote.
  • Confirm the service they want (Bath & Brush, Bath & Tidy or Cut & Style) and any add‑ons.
  • Check Gingr for available appointments and secure a time slot. If you’re unsure, put the request on a callback list and inform Leila.
  • Review our policies: no dogs over 80 lbs, no German Shepherds, Huskies or Golden Retrievers for haircuts, and proof of rabies vaccination required.
  • Ensure notes include temperament, health conditions and special requests.
  • Confirm the client understands that final pricing may vary after Leila evaluates the dog in person.
  • Send the client a confirmation email with appointment details and arrival instructions.

Service Details

Here’s what each grooming package includes:

  • Bath & Brush: Bath with premium shampoo, conditioning treatment, blow dry, full brush‑out, nail trim and ear cleaning. Ideal for upkeep between haircuts.
  • Bath & Tidy: All of the above plus trimming around the face, feet and sanitary areas. Keeps your dog neat without a full haircut.
  • Cut & Style: Complete grooming service that includes bath, blow dry, brush‑out and a breed‑standard or customised haircut. Great for seasonal coat changes or style transformations.

Add‑On Services

Add‑ons enhance the grooming experience or address specific needs. Here are explanations for each:

  • Paw and Nose Balm: Soothes and moisturises dry pads and noses with a natural balm.
  • Paw Fizz Treatment: A bubbly paw soak to clean, deodorise and soften paws.
  • Specialty Shampoo: Choose from hypoallergenic, whitening or de‑shedding formulas tailored to the coat.
  • Dental Care Package: Includes tooth brushing and breath spray to help maintain oral health.
  • Ear Plucking/Cleaning: Removes excess hair from ear canals and cleans ears to reduce odours and infections.
  • Gland Expression: Expresses anal glands to relieve discomfort and prevent impaction (for dogs that need it).
  • Nail Grind: Smooths and rounds nails after trimming for a polished finish.
  • Intense Treatment Mask: Deep conditioning treatment to nourish dry or damaged coats.
  • Clean Face: Thorough cleaning of the muzzle and beard area, including stain removal.
  • Clean Feet: Shaves pads and trims fur between toes for hygiene and traction.
  • Spoiled Puppy Spa: A gentle puppy‑friendly package with extra cuddles and calming scents.
  • Seasonal Spa: Themed spa treatment with seasonal fragrances and accessories.
  • Shed‑less Package: Uses de‑shedding tools and shampoo to reduce undercoat and loose hair.
  • Soothing Honey Spa: Honey‑infused shampoo and conditioning for sensitive or itchy skin.
  • Skunked: Special deodorising treatment to neutralise skunk odour.
  • Flea and Tick Treatment: Bath with medicated shampoo to kill and repel parasites.
  • Dematting: Removes mats and tangles; subject to additional time charges if extensive.
  • Express Fee: Prioritises your dog’s appointment for a quicker turnaround time.
  • Pampered Paws: Deluxe foot treatment with scrub, massage and moisturising balm.

Tips & Reminders

  • Always verify weight and breed restrictions before scheduling. Dogs over 80 lbs or certain breeds may require special handling or may not be accepted.
  • For double‑coated breeds (e.g. Huskies, German Shepherds, Golden Retrievers), recommend de‑shedding instead of shaving. Shaving can damage their coat and affect their ability to regulate temperature.
  • Encourage clients to book recurring appointments to maintain a healthy coat and avoid matting.
  • If a dog is severely matted, inform the client that additional dematting fees apply and that Leila may need to clip shorter than desired for the dog’s comfort.
  • Be transparent about pricing. Use the estimator as a guide, but remind clients that final cost depends on coat condition, size and temperament.
  • Use calm, positive language and avoid veterinary jargon when explaining services. Empathy builds trust.
